My name is Haeeun (Mina) Yun and I am currently a junior at Canyon Crest Academy. When my family and I first moved to the US from South Korea in 2014, I was suddenly pushed into an unfamiliar environment, one where my native language became useful only back at home. As a first grader joining her class just a few months before the school year ended, I often felt excluded and cut out from the rest of my classmates. With the language barrier and my reserved personality, it was especially difficult to form friendships or even communicate with my teacher. Growing up in a white-majority community also certainly did not help with my confidence or self-esteem, as I was frequently questioned by my peers about the lunches my mother packed me and my distinct accent. Though these matters may not seem all too big of a deal today, it was undoubtedly one of the most worrisome times of my early childhood.
